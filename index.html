The Volor Savanna Story

From Minecraft And Beyond!

Discovering Minecraft

It started in Summer 2013. I was watching a YouTube video by Blitzwinger about Minecraft, more specifically, the first BlitzCraft. In one video he was talking about wanting to make an underwater tunnel which the latter thought was cool. So I asked his Dad if he could play Minecraft.

This was before Minecraft Bedrock and before Microsoft bought Mojang. This was right before the horse update. His dad said yes. So his dad downloaded the Minecraft Java demo onto the family computer. At the time it had unlimited hours of play time and your gamer tag was simply “Player.”

It was later in Autumn that my brother was playing Minecraft Xbox 360 Legacy Edition demo. He thought the game was very stupid. Then one day in 2013, his brother saw me playing Minecraft on PC so he freaked out.

“Dad, Dad, I want to play Minecraft on the computer right now!”

And so he did. I asked for the Lego Coast Guard Patrol ship which he got. During Christmas day when my dad went to pick him up he said he had a surprise for them. My sister and I were confused. He had gotten them each (three in total) copies of Minecraft.

The Beginning of Coding

Fast Forward to about 2014-2015 and his dad bought the family a Python programming book. I tried creating a python script, hello world, and went to double click the .py file. No matter what he did, it would not run so he gave up on python. Then he realized Minecraft can be modded so he looked up what language Minecraft was coded in. It was a programming language called Java.

It was later at my Grandfathers that he went to watch a Netflix documentary that would change his life forever. “Rise of the Hackers,” by Nova. In it, they were talking about a cyber weapon, Stuxnet. The pure sophistication of Stuxnet fascinated me.

In 9th grade Autumn in health class there was a student that we will name Flish. He told the class he knew C++ and Python. Inspired by this, I set out to learn Java.

There was one problem, he did not have a laptop or computer. His grandfather had a Early 2015 MacBook Pro and let me use the Mac but was told that he could not download the Java JDK. So he looked at what he could make that was built into MacOS. He found a language by the name of AppleScript, not to be confused with Swift. He wanted something simple.

So he coded the NLT bible Chapter one of Genesis without his grandfather's permission. He would finish it by December of 9th grade with a combination of his grandfather’s MacBook Pro and his Mom’s Core 2 Duo macbook.

He would later realize that he can code in Java on the public libraries computers and so he did.

The Beginning of Volor Savanna

It was around January of 9th grade that he had two important dreams, one was where I was a spy escaping Russia. He heard the word Volor; which he would later learn that volor is similar to valor which means bravery so he liked it. He tried making a spy text adventure but gave up after he could not figure out the logic.

He also had a dream of a lion in a Minecraft savanna biome called The Mighty Lion. Now I do not remember whether or not the term “Mighty Lion” was from the dream and if it was or was not where it came from. Possibly from Power Rangers Jungle Fury.

It was around Spring of 9th grade that he figured out how conditional statements worked so he started work on his first video game, Volor Savanna, a text adventure.
